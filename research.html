Research

Advancing medical physics through innovative technology and clinical applications

My research program focuses on developing and implementing cutting-edge technologies to improve cancer treatment and medical physics education. Through interdisciplinary collaboration, I work at the intersection of physics, computer science, and clinical oncology to create practical solutions that enhance patient care and training quality.

AI & Large Language Models in Radiation Oncology

Developing privacy-preserving language model agents for autonomous radiotherapy treatment planning. The DOLA (Dosimetry Optimization with LLM Agents) project represents a groundbreaking approach to automated treatment plan optimization while maintaining strict patient data privacy standards.

Key Projects

  • Privacy-Preserving LLM Agents: Development of autonomous agents capable of optimizing radiotherapy treatment plans without compromising patient data security
  • Reasoning LLMs for Stereotactic Radiosurgery: Collaborating with University of Michigan to evaluate advanced language models for SRS planning applications
  • Clinical Implementation: Working toward integration of AI-assisted planning tools into clinical workflows at Henry Ford Cancer

Impact

This research has the potential to significantly reduce treatment planning time while improving plan quality consistency. The privacy-preserving approach addresses critical concerns about patient data security in AI-assisted medical applications.

MR-Guided Adaptive Radiotherapy

Advancing the field of MR-guided radiation therapy through development of novel dosimetry techniques and implementation of water calorimetry for absolute dose measurements. This work earned the 2021 Sylvia Fedoruk Prize for best Canadian medical physics paper.

Key Projects

  • Water Calorimetry in MR-Linac: Direct measurement of absorbed dose and absolute determination of chamber correction factors in magnetic fields
  • MR-Compatible Instrumentation: Design and validation of specialized dosimetry equipment for use in high magnetic field environments
  • Reference Dosimetry Protocols: Establishing best practice guidelines for dosimetry in 1.5T MR-linac systems
  • Clinical Optimization: Site-specific diffusion weighted imaging optimization for improved treatment planning

Impact

This research provides critical infrastructure for accurate dose delivery verification in MR-guided radiotherapy systems, improving patient safety and treatment outcomes. The water calorimetry work represents a gold-standard approach to absolute dosimetry in novel treatment platforms.

Virtual Reality Training Systems

Creating immersive virtual reality training simulators to enhance education in radiation oncology. These systems provide safe, repeatable training environments for complex procedures without patient risk or equipment access requirements.

Key Projects

  • Linear Accelerator Safety Training: VR simulator for teaching radiation safety protocols and linac operation procedures (CNSC Innovation Grant, $16,000 CAD)
  • Prostate Brachytherapy Simulator: Interactive VR environment for training radiation oncology, medical physics, and radiation therapy trainees in interventional procedures (TOLMAR Grant, $20,000 CAD)
  • Needle Insertion Module: Haptic feedback system for realistic procedural training
  • Educational Assessment: Quantitative evaluation of simulator effectiveness in skills transfer and learning outcomes

Recognition

This work was recognized with the 2023 AAPM Global Rising Star in Medical Physics award and was selected as an Arthur Boyer Education Symposium finalist. The VR training platform has been presented at multiple national and international conferences.

Impact

VR training systems democratize access to high-quality procedural education, allowing trainees to practice complex procedures repeatedly without patient exposure or equipment availability constraints. This technology has particular value for low-resource settings and standardized competency assessment.

Radiation Dosimetry & Detector Development

Developing advanced radiation detectors and dosimetry techniques for improved measurement accuracy in complex radiation fields. This includes novel approaches to linear energy transfer (LET) detection and characterization of detector response.

Key Projects

  • Doped Plastic Scintillators: Investigating high-Z doping effects on scintillator response for LET detection and energy spectrum resolution
  • Ionization Chamber Development: Monte Carlo optimization of prototype chambers for MR-guided radiotherapy applications
  • Radiobiological Dosimetry: Evaluating biological impact of scattered radiation and microdosimetric variations in brachytherapy
  • DNA-Based Detectors: Assessing DNA damaging effectiveness of ionizing radiation using plasmid DNA as a biological dosimeter
  • Novel Radionuclides: Monte Carlo calculation of microscale absorbed dose for emerging therapeutic isotopes (e.g., 119Sb)

Recognition

Dosimetry research has been recognized with the Anthony J. MacKay Best Student Paper Award (2018) and has been funded through competitive research grants including the Harold E. Johns Medical Physics Studentship.

Impact

Advanced dosimetry techniques enable more accurate dose measurement in challenging clinical scenarios, improving treatment delivery precision and patient safety. This work contributes to the development of next-generation radiation measurement technologies.
