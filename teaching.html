Teaching & Mentorship

Educating the next generation of medical physicists and radiation oncology professionals

Teaching Philosophy

I believe that effective medical physics education requires a balance of rigorous theoretical foundations and hands-on practical experience. My teaching approach emphasizes critical thinking, problem-solving, and the development of skills that translate directly to clinical practice.

Through innovative educational technologies like virtual reality simulators and interactive learning modules, I work to create engaging learning experiences that prepare students for the complexities of modern radiation oncology practice. I am committed to fostering inclusive learning environments and mentoring students from diverse backgrounds.

Curriculum Development

Patient-Specific QA Rotation Curriculum

University of Toronto - Medical Physics Residency Program (2023)

Developed comprehensive three-week rotation curriculum for medical physics residents covering patient-specific quality assurance in radiation therapy. The module includes:

  • Identification of learning objectives and competency milestones
  • Design of didactic and practical learning experiences
  • Integration with various stakeholders (radiotherapists, physicists, physics associates)
  • Development of evaluation methods and assessment tools
  • Creation of structured deliverables aligned with CAMPEP requirements

Virtual Reality Training Platform

Leading development of VR-based educational modules for radiation therapy training, including:

  • Linear accelerator safety and operation procedures
  • Prostate brachytherapy interventional techniques
  • Needle insertion skills with haptic feedback
  • Quantitative assessment tools for competency evaluation

These innovative training tools enhance accessibility to high-quality procedural education and enable standardized competency assessment across training programs.
