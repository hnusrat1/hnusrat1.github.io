About

Dr. Humza Nusrat is a Senior Staff Physicist at Henry Ford Cancer in Detroit and holds assistant professor positions at Michigan State University and Oakland University. His research advances the frontiers of medical physics through innovative applications of artificial intelligence, MR-guided radiotherapy, and virtual reality training systems.

With a Ph.D. in Biomedical Physics from Toronto Metropolitan University and CAMPEP certification, Dr. Nusrat's work bridges cutting-edge technology and clinical practice. He was recognized as the 2023 AAPM Global Rising Star in Medical Physics for excellence in emerging medical physics research, and received the prestigious 2021 Sylvia Fedoruk Prize for the best Canadian medical physics paper. His research has been supported by over $36,000 in competitive funding as principal investigator, resulting in 13 peer-reviewed publications, 1 book chapter, and more than 28 conference presentations.

Dr. Nusrat is deeply committed to advancing medical physics education and mentoring the next generation of researchers. He teaches in multiple CAMPEP-accredited programs, has supervised graduate and undergraduate research students, and develops innovative training technologies including virtual reality simulators for radiation therapy procedures.
